How to read the balance sheet in 5 minutes

1

Set the Stage: Consolidated vs Standalone

Start by comparing entity-level health to group-level health to uncover hidden parent-subsidiary risk transfers.
2

Look Back to Look Forward

Pull the 5-year trendline. Sudden spikes in liabilities or steady declines in asset quality are your first true warning signs.
3

Test the Solvency Floor

Check if working capital can cover immediate obligations without relying on next quarter's projected revenue.
4

Scan for Hidden Debt

Analyze off-balance sheet liabilities and short-term debt spikes that could drain liquidity during a downturn.

Hidden red flags to spot

Over-Leveraged Operations

How to spot it:

Check the Debt-to-Equity ratio. If debt far outweighs equity over multiple years, the company is highly vulnerable to interest rate hikes.

Off-Balance Sheet Tricks

How to spot it:

Toggle between Standalone and Consolidated views. Some entities hide risky liabilities in subsidiaries that don't appear on the standalone statement.

Illiquidity Disguised as Growth

How to spot it:

Compare Current Assets to Current Liabilities. A ratio consistently under 1.0 means they might struggle to pay short-term bills, even if sales are booming.
