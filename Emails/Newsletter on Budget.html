UNION BUDGET 2026–27

What actually changes for Indian SMEs

Union Budget 2026–27 introduces structural changes that affect how Indian SMEs grow, operate, and finance themselves.

 

The Impact Flows Through Three Levers:

□
Balance sheets
Equity enters the SME layer
The ₹10,000 crore SME equity fund reduces reliance on debt & eases pressure on promoter B/S while strengthening the SME net worth.
□
Working capital
Money moves faster after sale
The Budget improves post-sale cash flows by speeding up invoice realization through TReDS, enforceable payment timelines, & collateral-free financing.
□
Platforms
Demand becomes financeable
Key platforms are now integrated by linking Gov. Marketplace with TReDS to pre-verify government orders & cut paperwork & follow-ups for SMEs.

Manufacturing takes centre stage

The ₹12.2 lakh crore public capex allocation signals a long-term commitment to building domestic manufacturing capacity.

This translates into sustained demand for SMEs across priority sectors:

Electronics components
Semiconductors
Biopharma
Chemicals
Textiles
Capital goods
“As capital becomes cleaner and more institutional, clarity on company fundamentals matters more than ever.”

What this Budget really does

MSME policy used to focus on relief.
This Budget focuses on outcomes.

Three clear priorities follow.

Stronger balance sheets • Faster working capital • Long-term manufacturing

That’s how SMEs grow into stable, mid-sized enterprises.
